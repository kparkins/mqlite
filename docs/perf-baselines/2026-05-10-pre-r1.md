## PR0 decision-gate (plan §3 PR0)

The plan compares per-writer-partitioned same-namespace throughput
against multi-namespace throughput at equal writer count to test the
working hypothesis that `same_ns_single`'s slowdown is leaf-page-latch
contention:

```
R = same_ns_partitioned@4 / multi_ns_single@4
  = 3 844.57 / 10 057.52
  = 0.3823
```

**`R < 0.5` → ESCALATE before PR0.5 / PR1.**

Per the plan's middle-band rule:

> If `>= 0.85`: partitioning closes the gap, PR1+ premise validated.
> If `< 0.5`: bottleneck is broader than per-leaf latches, escalate
> before PR0.5/PR1.
> Middle band 0.5–0.85: PR1 / PR2 success ACs scale linearly to
> `min(stated_AC, normalized_ratio × stated_AC)`.

R lands materially below 0.5 — partitioning by `_id` range across four
writers in one B-tree gets us NOWHERE NEAR the four-namespace ceiling.
In fact `same_ns_partitioned@4` is lower than `same_ns_single@4`
(3 844 vs 4 166 docs/s). This is a load-bearing finding for the rest of
the plan: the rev-4 page-latch hypothesis is at minimum incomplete and
possibly wrong about the dominant symbol on this hardware.

What partitioning was supposed to remove (per the rev-4 reasoning):
contention on the rightmost leaf's exclusive latch. Each writer in the
partitioned axis writes monotonically into its own disjoint key range
(`writer_idx × 2³² .. writer_idx × 2³² + N`), so by construction the
hot leaves never overlap. If page-level latches were the dominant
serialization, `same_ns_partitioned` should approach
`multi_ns_single` (each NS = one B-tree).

What the data actually shows: same-NS hits a hard ~4 K dps ceiling
that does **not** scale with writer count (4w = 4 167, 8w = 3 937 —
goes DOWN), and which is also where workload partitioning lands. The
ceiling is set by something shared across same-NS writers regardless
of which leaf they touch. Candidates (in order of "looks load-bearing
on this profile"):

1. **MVCC frontier / publish gate.** All commits in one namespace
   advance a single visibility horizon; bonded `flip_pending_to_committed_for`
   work is serial per namespace. Multi-NS escapes by having one
   per-NS frontier.
2. **B-tree internal-node latches.** Even with disjoint leaves, all
   four writers traverse from the shared root, and right-edge inserts
   climb to internal nodes that share latches. Per-NS escapes since
   each NS has its own root.
3. **Buffer-pool pin/latch primitives** (the existing rev-4
   hypothesis). `pin_then_latch` 6 849 / `lock_exclusive_slow` 4 305
   / `Arc::make_mut` 4 093 are real on this profile (see
   [`2026-05-10-pre-r1-hot.md`](2026-05-10-pre-r1-hot.md)) — but if
   they were the *dominant* symbol, partitioning would have helped.
   They might be downstream of (1) or (2).
4. **Per-NS publish queue** in `paged_engine`. Contended on every
   commit in the namespace.

The hot-path sample table (`2026-05-10-pre-r1-hot.md`, captured
concurrent with another perf_axis run so absolute counts are inflated
by ~2x but ranking is intact) confirms (3) is real but does not
arbitrate between (3) being the root cause vs being a symptom. PR1's
selective-CoW + Phase-A/B redesign would still help (3), but the
4 K-dps ceiling means even a perfect (3) fix on this hardware can't
push `same_ns_single@4` past the same-NS ceiling unless (1)/(2)/(4)
are also addressed.

## Calibration note for downstream PRs

The rev-4 plan quotes `baseline_raw = 3 021 docs/s` from a
`perf/w1-unify-journal-allocation`-branch capture on different
hardware, and derives PR1's "≥ 3 625 docs/s" target from it. On this
M3 Pro the measured `baseline_raw = 4 166.49 docs/s` (median, see
table). All compounding-delta ACs in the plan are stated as multipliers
on `baseline_raw`, so they auto-recalibrate; the absolute thresholds
do not. Concretely:

| PR  | Plan formula                                                        | Calibrated to baseline_raw = 4 166.49 |
|-----|---------------------------------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| PR1 | `baseline_raw × 1.20`                                                | ≥ 5 000 docs/s                       |
| PR2 | `max(baseline_raw × 1.50, post_PR1_baseline × 1.25)`                 | ≥ 6 250 docs/s raw, or `post_PR1 × 1.25` whichever larger |
| PR4 | `≥ 1.05× post_PR2_baseline`                                          | post-PR2 × 1.05                      |

If R < 0.5 escalation reshapes the headline, those numbers are also up
for revision.

## Reproduction

```bash
# build
cargo build --release --example perf_axis

# matrix capture (writes both .json and .md sidecars; 11×12×15s = ~33 min)
tools/perf/run_baselines.py \
    --runs 11 --seconds 15 \
    --branch perf/p0-baselines \
    --out docs/perf-baselines/2026-05-10-pre-r1.json

# hot-path capture (one 15 s sample run)
target/release/examples/perf_axis --axis same_ns_single --seconds 15 --writers 4 &
PID=$!
sample $PID 15 -file /tmp/sample-pre-r1.txt
wait
tools/perf/sample_hot.py /tmp/sample-pre-r1.txt > docs/perf-baselines/2026-05-10-pre-r1-hot.md
```

`perf_axis` bypasses its `Client::drop` checkpoint via
`std::process::exit(0)` to keep the runner's wall-time tractable; see
the commit message for `examples/perf_axis.rs` for the rationale. The
in-loop throughput measurement is unaffected.

## TL;DR for the team-lead

- 11 (axis, writers) rows captured at median-of-11; envelope ≤ 5%
  on 10/11 rows, slight overshoot on `multi_ns_single@8`. Median
  reliable on all rows.
- **`baseline_raw = 4 166.49 docs/s`** (`same_ns_single@4`,
  median-of-11) on M3 Pro. PR1 target therefore re-anchors at
  ≥ 5 000 docs/s, PR2 at ≥ 6 250 docs/s.
- **PR0 decision-gate FAILS R<0.5: same_ns_partitioned@4 = 3 844
  docs/s vs multi_ns_single@4 = 10 057 docs/s, R = 0.38**. Plan
  prescribes ESCALATE before starting PR0.5 / PR1: the bottleneck on
  this code/hardware is broader than per-leaf-latch contention. The
  hot-symbol profile (`pin_then_latch`, `lock_exclusive_slow`,
  `Arc::make_mut`, `lock_shared_slow`) is real but apparently
  downstream of a same-NS-wide ceiling that workload partitioning
  cannot relieve.
- Recommendation to team-lead: pause PR0.5 entry until the escalation
  resolves. Either (a) confirm the rev-4 page-latch hypothesis is
  still the right first PR despite R < 0.5 (with downscaled
  expectations), or (b) prepend an investigation PR that profiles
  same_ns_partitioned and identifies the actual ceiling source
  (root-node latching, MVCC publish gate, per-NS journal queue, etc.).
